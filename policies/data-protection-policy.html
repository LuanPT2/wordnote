Data Protection Policy

Last Updated: December 2024
Effective Date: December 2024

This Data Protection Policy outlines how Wordnote ("we," "our," or "us") protects and manages personal data in compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other relevant regulations.

1. Legal Basis for Processing

1.1 Lawful Basis

We process personal data based on the following legal grounds:

  • Consent: When you explicitly consent to data processing
  • Contract Performance: To provide our language learning services
  • Legitimate Interest: To improve our services and prevent fraud
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with applicable laws and regulations

1.2 Data Processing Purposes

  • Service delivery and personalization
  • User authentication and account management
  • Learning progress tracking and analytics
  • Customer support and communication
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Legal compliance and reporting

2. Data Protection Principles

Principle Description Implementation
Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency Data processing must be lawful, fair, and transparent Clear privacy notices and consent mechanisms
Purpose Limitation Data collected for specific, legitimate purposes Data minimization and purpose specification
Data Minimization Only collect data that is necessary Regular data audits and minimization reviews
Accuracy Keep personal data accurate and up-to-date User data correction mechanisms
Storage Limitation Retain data only as long as necessary Automated data retention and deletion
Integrity and Confidentiality Ensure appropriate security measures Encryption, access controls, and monitoring

3. Data Security Measures

3.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: End-to-end encryption for data in transit and at rest
  • Access Controls: Role-based access with multi-factor authentication
  • Network Security: Firewalls, intrusion detection, and monitoring
  • Data Backup: Regular, encrypted backups with secure storage
  • Vulnerability Management: Regular security assessments and updates

3.2 Organizational Safeguards

  • Staff Training: Regular data protection training for all employees
  • Data Protection Officer: Designated DPO for compliance oversight
  • Privacy by Design: Data protection considerations in all processes
  • Incident Response: Comprehensive data breach response procedures
  • Vendor Management: Due diligence and contracts with third parties

4. Data Subject Rights

4.1 Right to Information

  • Clear information about data processing activities
  • Contact details for data protection inquiries
  • Regular updates on policy changes

4.2 Right of Access

  • Request a copy of your personal data
  • Information about processing purposes and recipients
  • Data retention periods and sources

4.3 Right to Rectification

  • Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Complete incomplete data
  • Update information through account settings

4.4 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")

  • Delete personal data when no longer necessary
  • Withdraw consent for data processing
  • Object to unlawful data processing

4.5 Right to Restrict Processing

  • Limit data processing in certain circumstances
  • Maintain data for legal claims
  • Verify accuracy of contested data

4.6 Right to Data Portability

  • Receive personal data in a structured format
  • Transfer data to another service provider
  • Direct transmission where technically feasible

4.7 Right to Object

  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Opt out of direct marketing
  • Object to automated decision-making

5. Data Processing Activities

5.1 User Account Management

  • Data Types: Name, email, password, profile information
  • Purpose: Account creation, authentication, personalization
  • Retention: Duration of account plus 2 years
  • Legal Basis: Contract performance and consent

5.2 Learning Analytics

  • Data Types: Progress data, quiz scores, learning patterns
  • Purpose: Personalized learning, progress tracking, recommendations
  • Retention: Duration of account plus 1 year
  • Legal Basis: Legitimate interest and consent

5.3 Communication Data

  • Data Types: Support messages, feedback, survey responses
  • Purpose: Customer support, service improvement
  • Retention: 3 years from last contact
  • Legal Basis: Legitimate interest and consent

5.4 Technical Data

  • Data Types: IP addresses, device information, usage logs
  • Purpose: Security, analytics, service optimization
  • Retention: 1 year from collection
  • Legal Basis: Legitimate interest and legal obligation

6. International Data Transfers

6.1 Transfer Mechanisms

  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers to countries with adequate protection
  • Standard Contractual Clauses: EU-approved SCCs for transfers
  • Certification Schemes: Privacy Shield successor frameworks
  • Binding Corporate Rules: Internal data transfer agreements

6.2 Safeguards for Transfers

  • Data protection impact assessments
  • Regular transfer mechanism reviews
  • Additional technical and organizational measures
  • User notification of transfer risks

7. Data Breach Response

7.1 Breach Detection and Assessment

  • Automated monitoring and alerting systems
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing
  • Incident response team activation procedures
  • Risk assessment and impact evaluation

7.2 Notification Requirements

  • Supervisory Authority: Within 72 hours of discovery
  • Data Subjects: Without undue delay if high risk
  • Internal Teams: Immediate notification to DPO and management
  • Documentation: Comprehensive breach documentation

8. Data Protection Impact Assessments

8.1 When DPIA is Required

  • Systematic monitoring of individuals on a large scale
  • Processing of special categories of personal data
  • Processing that could result in high risk to individuals
  • New technologies or processing methods

8.2 DPIA Process

  • Description of processing activities
  • Assessment of necessity and proportionality
  • Risk identification and mitigation measures
  • Consultation with data subjects and authorities

9. Third-Party Data Processors

9.1 Processor Agreements

  • Data processing agreements with all vendors
  • Clear specification of processing purposes
  • Security and confidentiality obligations
  • Regular audits and compliance monitoring

9.2 Vendor Categories

  • Cloud Providers: AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure
  • Analytics: Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude
  • Payment Processors: Stripe, PayPal, Apple Pay
  • Communication: SendGrid, Twilio, Intercom

10. Compliance Monitoring

10.1 Regular Audits

  • Annual data protection compliance audits
  • Third-party security assessments
  • Privacy impact assessments for new features
  • Staff training and awareness programs

10.2 Regulatory Cooperation

  • Cooperation with supervisory authorities
  • Timely response to regulatory inquiries
  • Implementation of regulatory guidance
  • Regular compliance reporting

11. Children's Data Protection

11.1 Age Verification

  • Minimum age requirement of 13 years
  • Parental consent for users under 16
  • Age verification mechanisms
  • Special protections for children's data

11.2 Parental Rights

  • Access to child's personal data
  • Right to delete child's data
  • Control over data processing
  • Notification of data breaches
GDPR Compliance: We are fully compliant with the General Data Protection Regulation and other applicable data protection laws. Our data protection practices are regularly reviewed and updated to ensure ongoing compliance.
Your Rights: You have comprehensive rights regarding your personal data. Contact our Data Protection Officer to exercise any of these rights or for more information about our data protection practices.
Data Security: We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal data. However, no system is 100% secure. We encourage you to use strong passwords and report any suspicious activity.
